A duck is a type of bird that belongs to the family Anatidae. They are generally found in aquatic habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds. Ducks have a broad, flattened bill, webbed feet, and a waterproof coat of feathers. They are well adapted to swimming and diving to catch their food, which typically consists of small aquatic creatures such as fish, insects, and plants. Ducks are also kept as domesticated animals for their meat, eggs, and feathers.
