A monkey is a type of primate, belonging to the biological family Simiidae. Monkeys are known for their intelligence, agility, and distinctive physical features, such as a tail, opposable thumbs, and forward-facing eyes. They come in a wide variety of shapes and sizes, with over 250 different species of monkeys found around the world. Some common types of monkeys include baboons, macaques, lemurs, tamarins, and marmosets. Monkeys are found in many different habitats, including forests, savannas, and mountains, and have played significant roles in many different cultures and religious traditions around the world.
